 What Happened To Abraham Lincoln’s Father?

Residing on the unforgiving Midwest frontier, Thomas Lincoln, a farmer and occasional cabinet maker, opted to raise his family.

As a stern and probably illiterate father, he struggled to comprehend Abraham’s aspirations for education, often scolding his son for prioritizing reading over chores.

Throughout Lincoln’s youth, especially following his mother’s death, the dynamic between father and son became strained.

Owing to Thomas’s diminishing eyesight and declining health, Abraham shouldered the responsibilities essential for maintaining the farm. Thomas passed away on January 17, 1851.

Why Did Lincoln Not Attend His Father’s Funeral?

Lincoln refrained from feigning grief or expressing affection he did not genuinely hold, leading him to abstain from attending his father’s funeral.

While not devoid of compassion, Thomas Lincoln symbolized a past world from which his son had long distanced himself.

 When Did Abraham Lincoln Die

Abraham Lincoln, the 16th President of the United States, passed away on April 15, 1865.

John Wilkes Booth, a renowned stage actor, assassinated him while he was attending the play “Our American Cousin” at Ford’s Theatre in Washington, D.C. Lincoln sustained a gunshot to the head and succumbed to his injuries the next day at 7:22 am in the Petersen House, located across from the theater.

This tragic event marked the first assassination of a U.S. president.

The nation observed an extended period of mourning during his funeral and burial.

Abraham Lincoln Wife

Mary Ann Todd Lincoln, the wife of President Abraham Lincoln, served as the First Lady from 1861 until her husband’s assassination in 1865.

Born into a wealthy Kentucky family, she was well-educated and courted by Stephen A. Douglas before marrying Lincoln.

The couple had four sons, with only the eldest, Robert, surviving them.

Mary actively supported her husband during his presidency, organizing social events at the White House, but her extravagant spending caused controversy.

She was with Abraham when he was assassinated in 1865.

The deaths of her husband and three sons took a toll on her, leading to physical and mental health issues.

Mary Lincoln died in 1882 at the age of 63, having faced depression and possible bipolar disorder.
