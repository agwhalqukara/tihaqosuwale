Kaelyn Petras is a well-educated person. She was a student of Notre Dame Preparatory School. She completed her graduation in 2006 with honors at the institution. She then enrolled at Michigan State University. She graduated with a Bachelor’s degree in Science and Zoology.

For further education, she attended the Ross University School of Veterinary Medicine Saint Kitts in 2010. As a result, she received a certificate in veterinary business management. Furthermore, she also graduated to become a Doctor of Veterinary Medicine.

Professional Career

Kaelyn Petras is a veterinarian and an animal doctor by profession. She has worked with different animals like dogs, cats, mice, rhinos as well as tigers. Her professional career began when she began working as a surgical assistant at the Goodison Veterinary Center.

She has also taught at the Ross University School of Veterinary Medicine. In addition, she has worked as an emergency veterinarian for a long time in organizations like the BluePearl Veterinary Partners and the 1st Pet Veterinary Centers.

After working for three years as an emergency veterinarian, Petras climbed up the positions and went on to handle the responsibility of an assistant intern director at the Arizona Veterinary Emergency and Critical Care Center. As of now, she is currently working as the director of emergency medicine at the Foothills Animal Hospital.
